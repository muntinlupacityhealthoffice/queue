ABOUT

The City Health Office (CHO) was created under Republic Act No. 7160, also known as the Local Government Code, where public health services were devolved to local government units. It is in charge of delivering health services to the community and implementing various programs from the Department of Health (DOH).

The CHO building is located at Centennial Avenue, Tunasan, Muntinlupa City. It has **15 health centers** and one health station distributed across the city's 9 barangays, with locations determined by the population of each area.

The CHO manages its personnel, coordinates with other government and non-government agencies to ensure basic health care delivery, and promotes community health. It also recommends ordinances to the Sangguniang Panglungsod through the Local Health Board to aid in the preservation and improvement of public health.

SERVICES

Death/Transfer Certificates & Exhumation Permits

The City Health Office handles the issuance of death and transfer certificates, as well as exhumation permits, which are signed by the government physician.

Water Laboratory Services

The City Health Office operates a water laboratory that functions as a testing center to determine whether water is safe for human consumption, especially for food establishments.

General Consultation & Treatment

The CHO provides general health services and implements various DOH programs, including:

  • National Tuberculosis Program
  • National Immunization Program
  • Maternal, Child Health & Nutrition
  • Rabies Program
  • Family Planning & Reproductive Health
  • Non-Communicable Disease Program

Medical Certificates for Employment

The City Health Office issues and signs medical certificates required by the Civil Service Commission for newly hired and promoted employees.

Issuance of Sanitary Permit

One of the functions of the City Health Office is to issue Sanitary Permits as a requirement under the Sanitation Code.

Basic Laboratory Services

The City Health Office provides basic laboratory services in health centers, serving as community-based facilities that deliver essential public health services.
